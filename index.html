About Idukki

Discover the crown jewel of Kerala's high ranges

The Land of Misty Mountains

Nestled in the Western Ghats of Kerala, Idukki is a breathtaking hill station known for its rugged mountains, dense forests, and sprawling spice plantations. The name Idukki is derived from the Malayalam word 'Idukku' meaning a narrow gorge, perfectly describing its topography.

Home to South India's highest peak - Anamudi, and Asia's largest arch dam - Idukki Arch Dam, this district offers a unique blend of natural beauty and engineering marvels. With altitudes ranging from 2,500 to 5,000 feet above sea level, Idukki enjoys a pleasant climate throughout the year.

Idukki is a biodiversity hotspot with numerous wildlife sanctuaries and national parks, making it a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers. From trekking through misty mountains to wildlife safaris and spice plantation tours, Idukki promises unforgettable experiences.
